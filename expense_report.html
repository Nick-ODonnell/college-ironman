Option A — UNSW Sydney + Ironman Australia
Option A is the only scenario where Australia, the Ironman, the internship, and a clean training timeline all coexist without a major sacrifice. At $24,518 it's only $300 less than Option B — but that $300 difference masks a much larger tradeoff: Option B puts peak Ironman training directly on top of senior spring semester, whereas Option A keeps the build clean all the way through the summer and into Sydney.

The moves that make Option A work: rent a bike in Australia rather than shipping, build a 2-week disruption buffer into the September move-in, and treat the Port Macquarie race as the fixed endpoint that organizes everything else. The race being 4 hours from Sydney is a feature — cheap travel, stays within your base.

Option C is the right contingency if the UNSW path falls through for any reason. Cleanest training setup, same internship, Lake Placid with family, and $3,000 cheaper than either Sydney option. Keep it ready.

Option D only makes sense if you're genuinely undecided about the Ironman. Don't choose it just to defer — post-graduation is harder to train for a full distance, not easier.
