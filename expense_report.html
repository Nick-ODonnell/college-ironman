Recommendation
Based on your stated priorities
Option A — Sydney + Ironman Australia
Option A is the only scenario where everything you actually care about fits without major compromise: the Australia experience, the Ironman, a summer internship, and a clean training timeline. The net cost (~$6,200 after internship income) is significantly lower than Option B, which sacrifices the internship and compresses training into your hardest academic semester.

The key moves that make Option A work: rent a bike in Australia rather than shipping yours, build in a realistic 2-week disruption buffer around your September move-in, and treat the internship as genuinely important rather than a checkbox — it serves you and your parents' concerns simultaneously.

Option C is the right answer if Australia genuinely doesn't happen for any reason. It has the cleanest training setup, the internship, and the cheapest cost. Keep it as your contingency.

Option B is the most expensive path to the same Australia experience — avoid it unless the Lake Placid race is specifically meaningful to you beyond just completing an Ironman.
